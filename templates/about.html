Our Story

Built because the best hackathon we wanted didn't exist.

The founders of Parallax had all attended hackathons — the good ones and the forgettable ones. The pattern was consistent: put engineers in a room, give them 48 hours, and watch them optimize around the edges of known problems. Nothing wrong with that. But it wasn't enough.

In 2022, a group of builders across machine learning, clinical research, architecture, and product design ran a small experiment: 72 hours, no tracks, mixed teams only. The results were strange and interesting. One team built a triage system using camera hardware repurposed from industrial quality control. Another borrowed a data compression approach from acoustic modeling.

Parallax was the formalization of that experiment — organized by SENSE at VIT Chennai, it has grown from 200 participants to a global event in just three years.
